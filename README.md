# US-Electric-Vehicle-Market-Share-Analysis


This project analyzes EV (Electric Vehicle) adoption across all U.S. states using Tableau, highlighting geographic disparities, alternative fuel usage, and infrastructure gaps. The dashboard identifies EV leaders and laggards, quantifies national adoption patterns, and provides data‑driven recommendations for infrastructure investment.

# 🎯 Project Objective 
The goal of this project is to understand how EV adoption varies across states, compare leaders and laggards, evaluate alternative fuel availability, and identify where infrastructure strain is emerging. It also demonstrates Tableau skills including KPI creation, mapping, bar charts, filters, and dashboard design.

The dashboard includes a KPI summary bar, EV leader–laggard comparisons, a national EV share map, an alternative fuels analysis, and a pie chart of top‑state EV distribution. Interactive filters allow deeper exploration, and a recommendation section highlights states with the greatest need for infrastructure investment.

# Business Questions 

1. What percentage of vehicles in each state are EVs, PHEVs, HEVs, and gasoline?

2. Which states have the highest EV adoption rates, and which states lag behind?

3. Which alternative fuels (biodiesel, ethanol, hydrogen) are significant vs. niche?

4. Where should policymakers prioritize EV infrastructure investment based on adoption trends and gaps in support?

# How I Built The Dashboard 
I began by reviewing the dataset to understand the structure, available fuel categories, and state‑level counts. I cleaned and standardized the data by checking for missing values, ensuring consistent state naming, and verifying that each fuel type was correctly categorized. I created calculated fields in Tableau to derive key metrics such as EV share and total alternative fuel usage.

To identify adoption patterns, I compared EV counts against other fuel types and calculated relative differences across states. I used ranking logic to classify states into EV leaders and laggards, highlighting geographic disparities and surfacing states that may require additional infrastructure or policy support.

I chose Tableau for this project because it supports intuitive geographic visualization and interactive exploration of state‑level trends. I designed the dashboard to guide users from a national overview into deeper comparisons, using maps, bar charts, and filters to make insights easy to interpret.

After building the visuals, I validated insights by cross‑checking trends across multiple charts and ensuring calculated fields aligned with the underlying data. I refined the color palette, labels, and tooltips to improve clarity and ensure the dashboard communicated insights accurately.


# 🧠 Business Insights and Findings 
EV adoption is highly uneven across the U.S., with California alone accounting for over one‑third of all EVs. Only a small group of states exceed the national average, while many rural states lag due to long travel distances and limited charging availability. Florida, Texas, and New York show high adoption but insufficient infrastructure, making them top priorities for investment.

# 🚀 Recommendation 
Florida, Texas, and New York should be prioritized for EV infrastructure investment because they combine large and fast‑growing EV populations with limited alternative‑fuel options, long‑distance travel gaps, or dense urban demand. Strengthening charging networks in these states would deliver the greatest near‑term impact.
